# Let's remember how our brain works... ![Brain model](img/full-brain.jpg) ----- # And there is one more interesting point... ----- # Why we do not remember everything? ![Library](img/full-library.jpg) ----- # Modern human memory model * **Storage capability** * Can handle huge amount of data * Degrades slow * **Retrieval capability** * Degrades fast * Relies on trained neural links > The main criteria the neural circuits use to decide when to alter themselves to accommodate new information is > *repetition*. The more frequently two or more unusual pieces of information co-occur, the more the brain decides they > must be important and prioritizes them for associative retention by altering neural circuits. The brain does not > really store "information" but rather adapts to statistical relationships among signals. >> `-- Paul King, fmr UC Berkeley Redwood Center for Theoretical Neuroscience` ----- # The forgetting curve ![The Forgetting Curve](img/full-forgetting-curve.jpg) ----- # Francis Bacon, 1620 > If you read a piece of text through twenty times, > you will not learn it by heart so easily as if you > read it ten times while attempting to recite from > time to time and consulting the text when your memory fails. ----- # Learning more ≠ Remember more ----- # Spaced repetition ![Spaced repetition](img/full-spaced-repetition.jpg)
